Kerala




    Capital: Thiruvananthapuram

    Major Festivals and Celebrations

     1. Onam

      Date: Thursday, August 12 - Monday, August 23

     Onam is a Godlike festival from God's own country.
     It is the time of the year when Kerala is exhilarated with trumpets, drums, elephants, boat races, dances, art, music, floral decorations, lights, colours, rituals and the delicious Onasadya.
     Onam is the traditional festival of Malayalees originated from the state of Kerala. Onam is a religious, cultural festival that's celebrated by the Malayali community around the world.


     2. Vishu

      Date: Wednesday, 14 April

     It is the Second most important festival of Kerala as it marks the New year which normally falls on the 14th, 15th, or 16th of April.
     The festival is marked by family time, preparing colourful auspicious items and viewing these as the first thing on the Vishu day (Vishukkani).
     The festival marks the first day of Medam, the ninth month in the solar calendar followed in Kerala.


     3. Thrissur Pooram

      Dates: April - May

     Largest and most famous of all Poorams, the Thrissur Pooram is held at the Vadukkannathan Swamy temple of Kerala every year in April - May.
     The temple-festival falls in the month of Medam of the traditional Malayali calendar, on the day when Pooram star witnesses the rising moon.
     The celebration takes place at the Vadakkunnathan temple which is located at a hammock at the centre of the city of Thrissur in Kerala.


     4. Thiruvathira

      Dates: December

     Thiruvathira or Thiruvathirai or Arudhra Darisanam is a Hindu festival celebrated in the Indian states of Kerala
     Thiruvathirakali is a dance form performed by women on the day of Thiruvathira to the accompaniment of Thiruvathira paattu, folk songs telling tales of lovesick Parvati.
     The sinuous movements executed by the group of dancers around a nilavilakku embody lasya or the amorous charm and grace of the feminine.
